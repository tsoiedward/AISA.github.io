AISA

AI Safety Asia (AISA) is a non-profit organization dedicated to reducing catastrophic AI risks by enhancing AI safety (AIS) and governance in Asia. It serves as a platform for intergenerational AI policy dialogue, connecting junior researchers and seasoned civil servants from Global South countries in Asia with senior AI safety researchers from the Global North.

Why AISA?

  • Southeast Asia lags behind in AI preparedness, creating an "algorithmic divide" that leaves the region vulnerable to AI's adverse impacts and missing out on its benefits.
  • Despite significant investments from the U.S. and China in Southeast Asian AI startups, local capacity and governance remain weak.
  • AISA addresses this gap by developing local talent and creating region-specific AI governance frameworks.

Our Goals

Enhance AI Safety Governance: Build local expertise and foster collaboration to produce practical AI safety policies.

Upskill Civil Servants: Equip mid-career civil servants with AI safety knowledge and tools to inform policymaking.

Incubate Junior Researchers: Develop junior policy researchers' skills through mentorship and practical policy input.

Foster Collaboration: Encourage cross-disciplinary and cross-regional collaboration to generate Asia-focused AI safety research.

Our Approaches

Training Programs: Conduct weekly AI safety tutorials for civil servants and mentorship sessions for junior researchers.

Collbaorative Projects: Establish teams of junior researchers, civil servants, and senior AI safety experts to produce practical AI safety policy research.

Mentorship: Provide ongoing guidance from established AI safety researchers to enhance junior researchers' capabilities.

Policy Development: Translate research outputs into practical policies through collaboration with local governments.
